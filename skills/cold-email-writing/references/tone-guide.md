# Tone Guide

Defines the voice and style for all cold outreach emails. Edit this when the
team's brand voice changes. The cold-email-writing skill re-reads it fresh on
every run.

## Core principles

1. **Lead with their world, not yours.** Open with something true about the
   prospect's situation. Don't open with your company name or a question
   about their pain.

2. **Be human, not polished.** Write like you're sending an email to a colleague
   you respect, not a brochure to a buyer. Contractions are fine. Short sentences
   are good.

3. **One ask.** Don't pitch and schedule in the same sentence. Pick one CTA and
   make it easy to say yes to ("15 minutes?" not "Would you be available for
   a 30-45 minute discovery call to explore synergies?").

4. **Short.** ≤ 150 words body. ≤ 60 characters subject. If you can't say it in
   150 words, you don't know what you're asking for.

5. **Specific > generic.** "Saw you raised a Series B last month" beats "I noticed
   your company is growing." Real signals only — never invent them.

## What to avoid

- "I hope this email finds you well." (or any variant)
- "I wanted to reach out to..." (why are you explaining your own action?)
- "Synergies", "leverage", "circle back", "touch base", "bandwidth"
- Three-paragraph setup before the ask
- Exclamation marks (especially in the subject line)
- All-caps words
- "Free", "guaranteed", "no risk" (spam triggers)
- Attaching a deck to a first email
- Two CTAs in one email

## Subject line rules

- State the reason for the email in plain language.
- ≤ 60 characters (most mobile clients truncate beyond this).
- No questions as subject lines for first outreach.
- No "Re:" or "Fwd:" if it's a cold email.
- Lowercase is fine; sentence-case is fine. Avoid Title Case For Everything.

## CTA formats (preferred)

- "Worth a 15-minute call?" (soft)
- "Happy to send a few notes if useful." (even softer — good for senior titles)
- "15 minutes this week?" (direct, minimal friction)
- "Can I send you a one-pager?" (good for a gated second step)

Avoid: "Let me know if you'd like to schedule a time..." (vague and passive).
